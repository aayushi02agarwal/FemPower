What can I do?

One of the most important things that you and others can do to help reduce period poverty and increase menstrual equity. You can start by learning about the people affected by period poverty. Menstrual products, such as pads and tampons, are getting increasingly expensive throughout the world, making them increasingly difficult to obtain for low-income women, homeless women, transgender individuals, and non-binary people. Therefore, many people develop poor menstrual hygiene as they are forced to use unclean clothes, newspaper or toilet paper to manufacture pads. These materials can cause infections in the reproductive and urinary systems. To help increase menstrual equity in our communities, we can donate pads, tampons, and other menstrual products to shelters, schools, and to jails. If we continue to educate ourselves and others on the effects of period poverty, we will become part of the solution.
